Just Use PostgreSQL

Stop overthinking it. It's literally a database that does everything.

What the f@%$ is PostgreSQL?

It's a relational database that's been around since 1996. That's longer than most JavaScript frameworks have been alive (RIP to the 47 frameworks that died while you read this).

It stores your data. It retrieves your data. It doesn't try to be your friend, your cloud platform, or your goddamn lifestyle choice.

Building a chat app? Use PostgreSQL.

"But I need real-time features!" you cry, reaching for Redis, MongoDB, Firebase, and seventeen other databases like you're collecting Pokémon cards.

PostgreSQL has LISTEN/NOTIFY. It's had it since before you were born. It does pub/sub. Real-time. Without making you learn a whole new freaking database.

Building an e-commerce site? Use PostgreSQL.

"I need to store products AND handle transactions!"

Yeah, that's called a FN ACID transaction, and PostgreSQL invented that.... Well, not invented, but it does them better than your ex did anything.

Orders, inventory, users, payment logs, shopping carts, wish lists, product reviews—all in one place. With foreign keys. Remember those? Data integrity? Or are we just raw-dogging data consistency now?

Building a social network? Use PostgreSQL.

"But graphs! I need a graph database for followers and connections!"

PostgreSQL has recursive CTEs. You can traverse your entire social graph faster than you can explain to your manager why you need Neo4j.

Posts, comments, likes, followers, notifications, DMs—it's all just fn rows. Stop making it complicated.

Building a geolocation app? Use PostgreSQL.

PostgreSQL + PostGIS = finding nearby pizza places without installing MongoDB just because it has "geospatial indexes."

It does points, polygons, radius searches, and probably your taxes if you ask nicely.

Need full-text search? Use PostgreSQL.

"I'll just add Elasticsearch!" you say, pulling out your credit card and your DevOps engineer's therapy bills.

PostgreSQL has built-in full-text search with tsvector and tsquery. It's not as fancy, but neither is your startup's search traffic of 12 users.

Need to store JSON? Use PostgreSQL.

"I need MongoDB for flexible schemas!"

PostgreSQL has JSONB. It stores JSON. It indexes JSON. It queries JSON faster than Mongo on a good day. And you still get all the relational stuff when you inevitably realize "flexible schema" means "CHAOS."

Need a cache? Use PostgreSQL.

"Redis is so much faster!"

Cool. You know what's faster than maintaining two databases? ONE DATABASE.

Use materialized views. Use unlogged tables. Use a SELECT query because you have 47 monthly active users and your "performance problem" is imaginary.

Need a queue? Use PostgreSQL.

RabbitMQ? Kafka? AWS SQS? Or... hear me out... a simple table with a status column.

SELECT FOR UPDATE SKIP LOCKED gives you a job queue. Done. No new infrastructure. No new AWS bill. No new database to learn.

Need time-series data? Use PostgreSQL.

"But InfluxDB! TimescaleDB! Prometheus!"

TimescaleDB is PostgreSQL, genius. And regular PostgreSQL handles time-series data just fine unless you're literally Google. You're not Google. Google doesn't use your SaaS boilerplate.

But what about scale?!

PostgreSQL scales to terabytes. Instagram used it. Apple uses it. Spotify uses it. They have more users than you have GitHub stars.

Your app with 300 concurrent users doesn't need sharding. It needs better indexes and maybe therapy.

But I like NoSQL flexibility!

"Flexible schema" is just "we didn't plan our data model" with marketing.

You know what's flexible? ALTER TABLE. You know what's not flexible? Debugging production data inconsistencies at 3 AM because your "schema-less" database let you store anything anywhere.

Fine, but when should I NOT use PostgreSQL?

When you're actually Google-scale. When you need sub-millisecond latency for millions of concurrent operations. When you're processing petabytes.

But you're not. You're building a to-do app with auth. Use PostgreSQL.
